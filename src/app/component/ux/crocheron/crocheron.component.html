Project Summary

The purpose of this study was to improve the usability of the Friends of Crocheron and John Golden Park website as the creators and users of this website believe that there are multiple roadblocks and challenges that need to be fixed. Throughout this study we have discovered several common issues and consolidated into four major problems, and provided corresponding recommendations to resolve these issues.

Background

The Crocheron Park is a 62 acres park located in Queens, New York with a long history. There are many regular visitors and there are many events hosted at this park every month. There are also many volunteering events happening periodically that allow anyone to participate to help improve the quality of the park.

Methodology

We recruited a total of 9 participants and conducted a remote usability test using a video conferencing tool Zoom. These participants are given a series of tasks to complete on the Friends of Crocheron and John Golden Park website, and we asks the participants to "think out loud" while they are navigating the website so that we can record their interactions and feedbacks.

Preparing Questions & Test

Before recruiting participants, we created a interest form containing 4 questions. This form is to get some general demographic information and the interest in the park from the users that we are going to be interviewing.
A series of tasks were then created to be asked when we conduct the interviews. The tasks will cover pretty much several important section of the website. The condensed version of the tasks and questions asked are below:

1

Is history something important to you? What would you like to learn more about the history of the park? Try to find this information on the website.

2

What kind of information would you expect to find in the park blog? What kind of content are you interested in reading about from the blog? Try going to the blog section and see which posts would you be interested to read more about.

3

Would you be interested in participating in an event? How would you like to participate in this event? Try to register as a volunteer for an upcoming event.

4

Why/why not would you be interested in donating to the park? Try to make a donation to the park

Participant Recruitment & Interview Scheduling

Our team tried to focus on users that actively go to the crocheron park, so we reached out to our client help us promote our research study during one of their events to get some participants to participate in our study. However, the number of participants that contacted us were quite low, so we also reached out to some students at the Pratt Institute as well.
Overall, we were able to recruit 9 participants ranging from 25 to over 60 years old. Most of the participants were working professionals or retired, while the others are students. Most participants are active users of the park and also have volunteered at the park before, while 50% of the participants have donated to the park in the past.

User Testing

After the participants filled out and submitted the interest screening questions, we scheduled all the participants and conducted our interviews. The tool that we used for this usabiliy test was Zoom, where the users will share their screen while they navigate through the website, with their camera and microphone on. As the participants going through the several tasks that we have given to them, we asked the participants to "think aloud", so we would be able to record and analyze users' interaction and reactions to the website.

Analyzing Results

There are several problems revealed after conducting all the user usability tests. After ranking each of the problems based on the severity and grouping duplicate and similar problems together, there are a total of 4 main problems identified. These problems cover the volunteering registration process, important information about donations on the donation page, the organization of the history page and format of the blog section.

Problem 1 - Volunteer Registration

Events and volunteering registration are one of the most important aspects of the park itself, but the process of registering was found to be somewhat confusing for many of the users. There are multiple ways for users to reach the registration page, and one of the links is broken, leading to a 404 page. Additionally, many of the users often go to the calendar page first to see what events are available, and they would expect the registration link to be available when they select a certain event on the calendar, which was found to be actually not clickable at all.
Recommendation:
A new simplified structure for the registration process as follow:
  • Create a single destination page for all the registration links from the homepage
  • Keep all the links consistent with the same destination webpage
  • Give two options once users clicks the registration button: Event registrations, Volunteer registrations
  • Users can register the events directly from the links in the calendar.
  • In volunteer registrations provide the form you already have in the website for general registrations

Problem 2 - Donation Page

When making a donation users find information about how the donations are utilized valuable, and would like to see it on the donation page. The current donation page only says “$10 to help support our cause!” but didn't indicate what the cause is. In addition, some users mentioned that they would like to donate to a certain project they are interested in and hope to see the progress of the project too.
Recommendation:
Based on the findings, the team came up with the following recommendations, providing information about the fund usage and intended use on the donation page by presenting information about how a particular donation category helps the park and providing a link to a detailed fund usage on the donation checkout page.

Problem 3 - History Page

It was observed that users were overwhelmed with the amount of information that was presented on the history page of the parks website. Moreover, the information was not efficiently structured on the history page. Users were confused with several aspects of the page including clickable buttons & pictures. Users kept scrolling through the page looking at the vast amount of information. Many users also missed key information about John Golden that was described in the starting text on the history page. Users needed streamlined guidance to access the information on this page.
Recommendation:
Make the following changes in the history page
  • Make the section buttons present on the top of history page more visually appealing by Increasing their size
  • Add clickable Photos
  • Add a timeline button as a history section that goes to the history timeline page

Problem 4 - Blog Page

Users found it difficult to search and discover blog posts. One user mentioned that she had participated in a park cleanup event and expected to find a post about that event in the blog but was unable to. Other users who were less familiar with the blog section of the site were inclined to scroll through the page to get a sense of what kind of content they could find in this section.
Recommendation:
Make the following changes in the history page
  • Order the posts by date in one column to adhere to the conventional blog format standards
  • Add a clickable archive and keyword filter section on the right side of the page to assist with search and discovery

Conclusion

In conclusion, the client was very happy with the results and recommendations that we provided. They said that these were the things that they felt deinitely need an improvment but not sure how to tackle the problems, and the recommendations that we have given were very helpful. If we were to continue to work on this project, I would like to interview more participants to get more opinions from other people as well. I would also like to do a follow up evaluation and eventually even have a redesign of the whole website to make it more attractive.
